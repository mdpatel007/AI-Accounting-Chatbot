A Profit and Loss statement, often called a P&L statement or income statement, 
summarizes the revenues, costs, and expenses of a business over a specific period of time.

It shows whether a business is making a profit or operating at a loss.

The key components of a Profit and Loss statement include:
- Revenue: income generated from business operations
- Cost of Goods Sold (COGS): the direct costs of producing goods or services
- Operating Expenses: salaries, rent, utilities, and other expenses
- Net Profit or Net Loss: the final financial result after all costs are deducted

The Profit and Loss statement helps business owners understand the financial performance 
of their business and identify areas where they can improve profitability.
