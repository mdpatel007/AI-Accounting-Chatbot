Cash flow refers to the movement of money into and out of a business over a period of time.

Understanding cash flow is important because even profitable businesses can face problems 
if they do not have enough cash available to pay expenses.

Cash flow is generally categorized into three types:

Operating Cash Flow – cash generated from normal business activities such as sales.

Investing Cash Flow – cash used for purchasing or selling assets like equipment or property.

Financing Cash Flow – cash related to loans, investments, or payments to shareholders.

A cash flow statement helps businesses monitor liquidity, manage expenses, 
and ensure they have enough funds to continue operations.
